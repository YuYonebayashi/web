Spain is a country located in southern Europe, mostly on the Iberian Peninsula. It has an area of about 506,000 square kilometres. The capital is Madrid. In addition to the Spanish mainland, Spain has the Balearic Islands in the Western Mediterranean, the Alboran Islands in the Alboran Sea, the Canary Islands in the Atlantic Ocean and the North African exclaves of Ceuta and Melilla.The name "EspaÃ±a" comes from the Phoenician word "i-shaphanim", meaning "island of the hyrax", which the Phoenicians mistook for the hare that inhabited the region. The population is about 46.93 million, about a third of the size of Japan. Of Iberian-Celtic descent, the Spanish people today are made up of Castilians, Galicians, Andalusians, Catalans and Basques. Most of the country's regions have a Mediterranean climate. The northern part of the country, from the Basque Country to Galicia, has a west coast maritime climate with high rainfall. The Canary Islands, to the south-west of the mainland, also have a subtropical climate. In the north, wheat and livestock are produced. The central part produces wheat, grapes and livestock products. The east produces citrus fruits and rice, while the south produces olives, grapes, vegetables and rice.
